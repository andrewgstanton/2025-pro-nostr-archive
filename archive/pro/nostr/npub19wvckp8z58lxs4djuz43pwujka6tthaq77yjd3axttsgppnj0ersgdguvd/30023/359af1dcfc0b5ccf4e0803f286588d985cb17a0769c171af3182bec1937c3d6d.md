Andrew G. Stanton - Sept. 26, 2025

Hope is not optimism. It’s the defiance of despair.

Optimism looks for reasons; hope looks for resurrection.  
One collapses when the evidence fades; the other grows stronger in darkness.

After loss, after death, after collapse — what remains is the quiet conviction that life itself is not finished with us yet. That somehow, even the ashes are holy.

The early Church knew this hope. So did the early Bitcoiners. Both faced ridicule, isolation, persecution. But they saw something eternal beneath the ruins of the age — something that could not be crucified twice.

Hope after the ashes is not naive. It’s prophetic. It believes what God has promised, not what man has built. It trusts that every fallen tower will someday give way to a City not made by hands.

So even as we walk through ruins — personal, cultural, economic — we do not mourn as those without hope.

Because the same fire that destroys also refines.
