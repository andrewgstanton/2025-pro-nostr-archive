## II. Physical Character  
Sovereignty interacts with physics in four observable ways:

### **1. Energetic Cost**  
Any attempt to impose external control requires measurable expenditure of energy.  
In nature, coercion is never free.

### **2. Locality**  
Systems act most effectively within their own local domain.  
Distance weakens influence — a principle echoed in quantum decoherence and interplanetary consensus lag.

### **3. Resilience Under Pressure**  
Structures aligned with sovereignty exhibit antifragility:  
they strengthen when attacked,  
harden when stressed,  
and adapt without breaking.

### **4. Conservation**  
Sovereignty can be suppressed temporarily,  
but not destroyed.  
It returns to equilibrium when constraints fail —  
just as physical systems return to lowest-energy states when force is removed.
